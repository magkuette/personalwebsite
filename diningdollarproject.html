Abstract

This program is intended to take user input from students at the University of Maryland in order to calculate information concerning their university dining dollars.

Background

Dining Dollars are a type of currency used at the University of Maryland. They can be used for paying for various services on campus such as getting food at the Student Union, STAMP, buying from the convenience stores, and more.
Students are able to receive a Dining Dollar Plan by specifying the type of plan they would like. Such plans typically consist of an amount of either $200 or $400. The money in students' accounts expires.
As of now, the University of Maryland (UMD) has a website and app called UMD GET which is used to display students' remaining university dining dollar balance and recent purchases.
However, a friend from UMD - Arnav Patel - and I realized this app and website was missing a few features that we felt students needed.

Project Concept

Arnav and I designed a supplement to the UMD GET app which takes user data to do any or all of the following

  • Create a list of dates a user should set a notification to spend their Dining Dollars
  • Calculate the amount of money the user has spent using quartile thresholds
  • Create a basic daily spending plan

Work Process

Arnav and I created the foundation of the program together and often met up to discuss and work on the project. We used GitHub to pass in each of our commits.
While working on the Dining Dollar Interface Add-On, Arnav was the driver of most of the back-end part including calculations of the Dining Dollars, while I was the driver of most of the front-end part including the placement of interactive user components on the screen using Tkinter, and error handling such as notifying the user when they have done something against the program's intention.
